Biography

I was born in Hanoi, Vietnam. I attended Vietnam National University from 2007 to 2011. I did my master course in cooperative program between Vietnam National University and University of Paris XI. Now I am a doctor student at The University of Aizu, Japan.

Research Interests

My favourite research is hardware-software co-design. I also pay attention on DSP, video/image processing and on-chip communication. My major is belong to hardware design so I mainly work with FPGA or ASIC tools.

University of Paris XI

In cooperation with University of Engineering and Technology
Hanoi, Vietnam 12/2011~4/2014
  • Graduation Thesis: “An Efficient VLSI design of H.264/AVC Inter-Prediction”
  • Advisor: Prof. Xuan-Tu Tran
  • Description: Thesis proposes an efficient architecture of Main Proﬁle H.264/AVC Inter-Prediction as a part of VENGME Project. This architecture supports variable block-size, bi-predictive and full-fractional motion estimation. Proposed design applies three main key ideas: full-search algorithm, bandwidth efficiency technique and pipeline coding ﬂow. Proposal is designed in VHDL, veriﬁed and implemented in 180 nm CMOS technology.

Vietnam National University

University of Engineering and Technology
Hanoi, Vietnam 09/2007~6/2011
  • Graduation Thesis: “Design and implementation of a Re-configurable Router for On-chip Networks”
  • Advisor: Prof. Xuan-Tu Tran
  • Description: Thesis aim to propose re-configurable router for NoC with two steps. First step, the normal router is built for wormhole 2D-Mesh topology Network-on-Chip model. Second step, a by-pass method for router is proposed to avoid router errors. Both of normal and reconfigurable router are designed in VHDL, verified and implemented in FPGA. The normal router model is presented in ICDV 2011.
