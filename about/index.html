About

Xin chào! Hello! こんにちわ！

My name is Dang Nam Khanh (Dang is my family name, Khanh is my first name). In Vietnamese (my country language), my name is Đặng Nam Khánh. My Japanese name (katakana) is ダン ナム カイン. You can call me Khanh, Khánh, カイン in English, Vietnamese and Japanese, respectively. Here is a figure of me while talking about my country: link.

I am a doctor student at the University of Aizu, Japan. I am an ASIC/FPGA/Hardware designer. I work at Adaptive Systems Laboratory to research about a High Reliable 3D Network-on-Chip & its Applications.

I am currently living in Fukushima Prefecture(福島県), a prefecture of Japan which is mostly known by a nuclear factory disaster (11th March 2011). I moved from Hanoi, Vietnam to Fukushima, Japan since September 2014 (3.5 years after the disaster). Some of my friends worry about this place but it seems like a second hometown to me.

I formerly was at SIS Lab (2009~2014) to develop an H.264/AVC Encoder. This project is the first video hardware encoder chip designed by Vietnameses. I developed the Motion Estimation/Compensation part.

TL;DR: I am Dang Nam Khanh, a PhD student. I am a Vietnamese, living in Fukushima, Japan. I am a hardware designer.
